1.2.1 Multiply and Divide Rational expressions

  • First find the numbers that will cause the denominator to not be zero
  • Can only eliminate factors(binomials) that are alike. Can't factor pieces
  • Ex. \(3(x-2)\) can only factor 3 or \((x-2)\)
  • factor everything in sight and then cancel out
  • Dividing: Use reciprocal and multiply

1.2.2 Negative one factoring

  • If the problem has \((5-x)\)
  • Force a negative one out of the factor(binomial)
  • Ex. \(-1(x-5)\)
  • If that happens in the denominator
    • Divide the numerator by the -1 once the factor is cancelled

1.2.3 Add and Subtract Rational expressions (algebraic fractions)

  • what is a rational expression? \(\frac{(2x)}{(2x-3)} - \frac{3x-5}{5x}\)
  • Find the LCM
    • The LCM is the simplest polynomial that contains all the factors of each polynomial
  • Ex. Find the LCM of \((4x^2+4x)\) and \((x^2+2x+1)\)
  • Factored form: \(4x(x+1)\) and \((x+1)(x+1)\)
  • LCM == \(4x(x+1)(x+1)\)
  • Finding the fraction with Common Denominator
    1. Rewrite the fraction so that the denominator equals the LCM
    2. That involves multiplying the denominator by the terms that make it equal the LCM
    3. Multiply the numerator by the same number from step 2
    4. Then add or subtract over the common denominator
    5. You may need to factor and/or cancel to get the right answer

1.2.4 Solving Rational Equations

  • Find the LCM
  • Multiply through all terms and look for cancellation opportunities
  • Factor the equation to zero
  • Verify that each solution does not set the denominator to division by zero

1.2.6 Complex Numbers

  • \(\sqrt{-9} = \sqrt{9(-1)} = 3i\)
  • \(\sqrt{-75} = \sqrt{25 * 3(-1)} = 5i\sqrt{3}\)
  • Adding and subtracting
    1. Simplify as much as possible
    2. Drop the parens and collect like terms
    3. Express the answer in \(ax+b\) form
  • Multiplying
    1. Distribute terms
    2. \(-3i(10-15i)\)
    3. \(-30i+45i^2\) and \(i^2 = -1\)
    4. \(-30i-45\)
    5. \(ax+b\) form = \(-45-30i\)
    6. If binomial, simply FOIL
  • Finding the value of \(i^n\)
    1. recall that $i = \sqrt{-1}
    2. Make sure calculator is in \(ax+b\) mode
    3. so \(i^5\) is simply \(\sqrt{-1}*\sqrt{-1}*\sqrt{-1}*\sqrt{-1}*\sqrt{-1}\)
    4. Answer is: \(i\)
  • Dividing
    1. Use a conjugate to clear the denominator
    2. \(\frac{4-5i}{2i}\) what will clear the denominator?
    3. in this case simply multiplying by \(i\) will do it
    4. in cases with a binomial use the conjugate as well
    5. Ex.\(\frac{4-5i}{3-7i} * \frac{3+7i}{3+7i}\)
    6. Make sure to simplify fractions and write in the correct form

1.2.7 Completing the square

  • Use this technique when we have a quadratic in \(ax^2+bx+c\)
  • write into this form \(ax^2+bx = -c\)
  • Take half of \(bx\) and raise it to the \(n^2\) power
  • so now we have \(ax^2+bx+\frac{1}{2}(bx)^2 = -c + \frac{1}{2}(bx)^2\)
  • then we have \((x+\frac{1}{2}(bx))^2 = cd\)
  • to clear the exponent on the binomial, square both sides
  • \(x + \frac{1}{2}(bx) = \sqrt{cd}\)
  • \(x = - \frac{1}{2}(bx) +- \sqrt{cd}\)
